Our Story

A sauce built around an idea.

Chicaoji wasn't dreamed up in a marketing room. It came together one summer on a small island in the Salish Sea, in a juice bar that took the words "food is medicine" seriously.

Lopez Island
Salish Sea, WA
Summer 2007

It started at the Vortex Juice Bar.

Chicaoji was created in the summer of 2007 by Randall Waugh, with no small amount of input from the community at the Vortex Juice Bar & Cafe on Lopez Island, Washington.

The original brief was simple: make a hot sauce that nourishes. Pick ingredients that earn their place in the bottle. Don't reach for filler. Don't add anything that doesn't belong.

What came out the other side was Chicaoji — three superfoods, a vinegar base, a kiss of maple syrup, a pinch of Celtic Sea Salt, and nothing else.

The guiding principle behind Chicaoji has never moved: Food is Medicine. The bottle is just one way of practicing that idea.

The Philosophy

"Food is Medicine" isn't a slogan. It's a working belief that every ingredient on a plate is doing something, for you or against you, and that a condiment isn't excused from that. So Chicaoji is built from foods chosen for what they do as much as how they taste.

Chipotles — smoked jalapeño chilies — bring warmth and complexity. Cacao, in its raw nib form, contributes the bitter depth and the long list of compounds people quietly love it for. Goji berries are the connective tissue: they add sweetness, color, and the body of one of the oldest tonic foods in continuous use.

"Great ingredients mean great taste. Everything Chicaoji needs is already in the name."

The Name

Chicaoji is a portrait of the bottle, condensed into a single word.

  • CHI — from chipotle, the smoky chili that anchors the heat
  • CAO — from cacao, the bitter depth in the background
  • JI — from goji, the sweet ruby-red lift

Three syllables. Three ingredients. Say it: Chick · OW · gee.

The Place

Chicaoji is made on Lopez Island, one of the San Juans in the Salish Sea, off the northwest corner of Washington State. It's a place where small, careful food has always been the default — and where a juice bar can become the birthplace of a sauce that ends up on tables across the country.

The Promise

Every bottle of Chicaoji is made the same way it was in 2007. No preservatives. No flavorings. No colorings. No additives of any kind. Just three superfoods, the things that hold them together, and the conviction that food, done right, is the closest thing to medicine most of us will ever have.

Taste the philosophy.

The fastest way to understand what Chicaoji is about is to put a drop on a piece of food and try it.
